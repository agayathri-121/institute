Inside a Space Station

Space stations are laboratories in orbit where astronauts live and work. They perform experiments in microgravity, observe Earth and space, and test technologies for future deep-space missions.

  • Microgravity experiments in physics and biology
  • Earth observation and climate monitoring
  • International collaboration between space agencies
  • Testing life support systems for long-duration spaceflight

Key Facts

Orbit Height: ~400 km above Earth

Speed: ~28,000 km/h (about 90 minutes per orbit)

Crew: Astronauts from different countries
